O

Offense

1

Hunt Open Space — Flash & Yell "BALL!" ★ Top priority

  • Lane opens up: flash to the paint hard, yell "BALL!" — be the read.
  • Catch in the lane: turn and finish, OR kick to the open shooter. Don't dribble back out.
  • Off the catch on the wing: if the gap is there, attack on the FIRST dribble.
  • Strong move, one direction: left OR right — commit. Hesi-cross into nothing = turnover.
2

Impose Your Will — Shoot

  • Open on the catch = SHOOT. You're 7-for-8. Putting it on the floor when open = wasted possession.
  • Closeout coming at you: ONE dribble into the pull-up. Not 2, not 3.
  • Rim attack every quarter: at least one. FTs are points the defense can't stop.
  • First shot in the first 3 minutes. Set the tone — don't disappear in Q1.
D

Defense

3

Stay Down — Length, Not Leaping

  • Pump fake: feet stay on the floor. Hands up, don't bite.
  • Real shot: contest STRAIGHT UP, don't drift into the shooter — no foul.
4

Don't Lose Your Man Off-Ball

  • See man AND ball: head on a swivel. Can't see both = you're wrong.
  • Get screened: FIND YOUR MAN FIRST, then locate the ball. Don't stand up to watch.
  • Sit down off-ball. Standing = late to everything.
5

Closeouts — Squeaky Shoes, Late Hands

  • Sprint, then chop: full sprint to close, short choppy steps (squeaky shoes) into the contest.
  • High-to-low: stay low approaching, hand UP late. Low feet beat the fake; late hands take the shot.
6

Box Out & Rebound

  • Shot up: find a body, hit first, THEN chase. No flying past your block-out.
  • Secure → push: if you grab it, locate quick — be the outlet or start the break yourself.
7

Talk On D — Tactical Calls

  • "Screen left!" / "Screen right!" — BEFORE your teammate gets hit.
  • "Switch!" or "Stay!" — decide on every screen. Silence = breakdown.
  • "Ball, ball, ball!" — after rotation, claim the ball.
  • "Shot!" — yell when ball goes up so everyone boxes out.
  • "Dead!" — dribble is dead, crowd him.
8

Lead With Your Voice — Lift the Team

  • Teammate misses a shot: "YEAH! Next one!" — pick him up immediately. No heads down.
  • Teammate turns it over: "My bad — get the next one!" Own it with him, sprint back.
  • Shot goes up and nobody's hitting bodies: "REBOUND! BOX OUT!"
  • Energy drops: be the one who lifts it. Clap, talk, point — visible, audible.
  • After a big play (yours or a teammate's): celebrate it loud. Energy is contagious.
  • Why it matters: college coaches scout for vocal leaders. Tape doesn't pick up X's and O's as well as it picks up who's running the floor.
Process drives the points. Cadence drives the consistency. Hunt Open Space leads.
